01 CONDUCTING RESEARCH
_
P R O B L E M

Due to the drastic transition to an independent lifestyle, students may quickly fall under the trap of developing unhealthy eating habits. It’s become increasingly common for students to prioritize academic or work responsibilities over health, and it’s not necessarily because they aren’t aware of the consequences.

U n d e r s t a n d i n g   O u r   U s e r s

We narrowed our search into three focuses:
1. How they feel about cooking homemade meals
2. Priorities when it comes to eating or eating habits
3. Obstacles with home cooked meals

T o p i c   R e s e a r c h

The first things we did was research. Emily read paper publications on health and eating habits of college students.

Common barriers to healthy eating

Time constraints, unhealthy snacking, convenience high-calorie food, stress, high prices of healthy food, and easy access to junk food

Factors that contribute

Individual (intrapersonal), social (interpersonal), university environment (community settings), and students' life factors as influences affecting eating habits

U S E R   S U R V E Y S   &   O B S E R V A T I O N S

We created a user survey and conducted 15 interviews among college students from both University of Florida and University of Maryland.

Key takeaways

  • 10 (66%) respondents tend to cook at home most each week
  • 10 (66%) respondents prioritize healthy eating
  • Obstacles respondents face when cooking at home: kitchen is too messy or too small, there's not enough time, lack of motivation, they feel too tired or lazy
  • 11 (73%) respondents sometimes aren't aware of the expiration of their food
  • If friends and family shared healthy eating habbits, 10 (66%) respondents would be influenced by their meal choices
  • 15 (100%) would like to track their groceries
Key Takeaways

Pain points are significant! And it was important to fully understand our users challenges and what Passionfruit can do to help

  • Target user 1 - Users who often times find it challenging to find time dedicated to cooking at home. They wish there was a way to keep them accountable and try dishes that take under 30 minutes to prepare.
  • Target user 2 - Users who would like to cook with ingredients they already have at home, so that they won't have to go to the grocery store several times a week to prepare their desired dish. They would like to see what's in their fridge and quick recipes that uses the same ingredients.

06 REFLECTION
_
P r o j e c t   L e a r n i n g s   &   R e f l e c t i o n

Although it was a tiring weekend, I thought it was actually really fun. Emily presented the idea and I thought that it may be really helpful to college students transitioning from high school or just trying to become healthy.

Communication is important!
I believe what made our project so successful was Emily and I's communication. After every task, we often updated eachother and made sure we were on the same page. We did not have a lot of misunderstandings - even if we did it was clarified quickly.

Determine the value of the app
The part that took up most of the time was determining what should and should not be in our app. From our research and surveys, we found many problems and lots of possible solutions. We had to narrow down to the most important and what really mattered for our target users. Test!
Unfortunately because there was a limit in time, we could not conduct usability tests as we had hoped. If we conducted some testing, we would know what works and does not work in terms of UI design and usability.
